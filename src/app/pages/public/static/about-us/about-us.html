About Us

1. Organizational Profile The MyCoral Eco-Marine Project (MyCoral) is a premier sustainability initiative established under the purview of the International Medical School (IMS) at Management and Science University (MSU). Aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 14: Life Below Water, the organization serves as a bridge between academic research, environmental stewardship, and community engagement. MyCoral operates with a dual mandate: to actively rehabilitate degraded coral reefs through scientific intervention and to foster long-term climate resilience through education. 2. History & Background MyCoral was founded in 2016 in response to the critical decline of marine biodiversity in the South China Sea. Recognizing that environmental health is intrinsically linked to public health, MSU expanded its academic scope to include planetary health. The project initially focused on the waters off Setiu, Terengganu, specifically Pulau Geluk, establishing it as a primary sanctuary for rehabilitation efforts. Over the past decade, the initiative has evolved from annual student expeditions into a comprehensive, multi-stakeholder program involving state government agencies, corporate partners, and international universities. 3. Key activities include: Artificial Reef Deployment: Engineering and deploying structures to stabilize the seabed and support coral polyps. Coral Propagation: collecting and replanting broken coral fragments to accelerate reef regeneration. Biological Monitoring: Conducting regular data collection on marine life density and water quality in collaboration with the Department of Fisheries Malaysia. B. Community Empowerment (The "Community Diver" Initiative) Sustainability requires local stewardship. MyCoral actively engages the coastal communities of Terengganu, transforming local fishermen and villagers into certified ecosystem guardians. By providing scuba diving certification and eco-tourism training, the program creates alternative economic opportunities that incentivize conservation over exploitation. C. Technological Innovation To bridge the gap between the ocean and the general public, MyCoral leverages technology for advocacy. The development of the MyCoral VR (Virtual Reality) module allows the public to experience reef ecosystems immersively, fostering environmental awareness without the logistical barriers of physical diving. 4. Recent Developments As of late 2025, MyCoral has been integrated as a foundational component of the newly launched MSU Centre for Climate Resilience and Strategy (m-CREST). This strategic elevation underscores the university's commitment to climate action, providing the project with enhanced resources to expand its footprint across the ASEAN region, including recent collaborative expansions into Indonesia.

Our Mission

To protect and restore coral reef ecosystems through innovative conservation strategies, scientific research, and community partnerships. We believe that healthy oceans are essential for a healthy planet.

Our Vision

A future where coral reefs flourish as vibrant, resilient ecosystems that support marine biodiversity and coastal communities worldwide. We envision oceans teeming with life and color.

What We Do

Conservation

Active restoration projects to rehabilitate damaged coral reefs and protect existing healthy ecosystems.

Education

Community programs and workshops to raise awareness about coral reef conservation and ocean health.

Adoption

Coral adoption programs that allow individuals to directly support reef restoration efforts.
